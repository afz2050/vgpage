Cost of Living and Expenditure in Malaysia

By Afras Ashraf (33803218)

Over the past decade, Malaysians have faced steadily increasing living costs. From basic groceries to housing and transport, price changes affect every household — but not all regions or income groups experience these changes equally.

This visualisation explores how prices, inflation patterns, and household affordability differ across Malaysia

Price Changes Across Categories

Not all goods and services have risen in price at the same rate.

You can observe that Food and Non-Alcoholic Beverages consistently remain the largest contributor to inflation, followed by Housing, Utilities, and Transport.

Inflation Trends Over Time

The line chart here shows Malaysia's CPI trend over time, representing the overall change in the cost of living

Urban vs Rural Differences

The cost of living gap between urban and rural areas is clearly visible in the CPI data.

Urban vs Rural Inflation

The specific groups that have the most inflation can be seen here.

While rural areas tend to have lower housing and service costs, urban residents face higher prices for transport, rent, and food.

Household Income

States such as Kuala Lumpur, Selangor, and Putrajaya record the highest household incomes, reflecting their more urbanised economies and higher-paying industries.

In contrast, Kelantan, Perlis, and Sabah have significantly lower average incomes, highlighting the continued urban-rural income gap across the country.

Household Expenditure

Spending patterns tend to follow income levels — households in urban states such as Kuala Lumpur, Selangor, and Penang spend considerably more per month, largely due to higher housing, transport, and food costs.

Meanwhile, rural and northern states such as Kelantan, Perlis, and Terengganu have lower overall expenditures, reflecting both lower living costs and lower disposable incomes.

Summary and Insights

Malaysia's overall CPI has steadily increased from 2010 to 2025.

Food, housing, and transport remain the primary cost drivers.

Urban households experience higher inflation rates than rural ones.

Data Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM)
